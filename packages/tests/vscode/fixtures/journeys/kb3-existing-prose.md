# Notes on Software Architecture

Software architecture is the high-level structure of a software system. It defines the components, their relationships, and the principles governing their design and evolution over time.

Good architecture enables teams to work independently on different parts of the system. Each component should have a clear responsibility and well-defined interfaces with other components.

Testing is an essential part of maintaining architectural integrity. Without proper tests, systems tend to degrade as developers make changes without understanding the full impact of their modifications.

Documentation helps new team members understand the system quickly. It should be kept up to date and focused on explaining the why behind decisions, not just the what.
